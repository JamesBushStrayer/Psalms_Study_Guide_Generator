Psalm 23, also known as the "Shepherd Psalm," is one of the most famous and beloved chapters in the Book of Psalms, which is part of the Old Testament in the Bible. Traditionally attributed to King David, it is believed to have been written around 1000 BC, during the Israelite monarchy period. This period was characterised by conflict but also spiritual growth. Set in a pastoral culture where shepherding was a common occupation, the psalm uses metaphors of God as a shepherd and his people as sheep. It outlines a profound sense of trust in God's protection and provision, making it a source of comfort and hope in times of personal trials and collective crises.**Psalm 23: The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want.**

2. Provider - The statement "I shall not want" shows God as a provider. The shepherd ensures that the sheep lack nothing, and in like manner, God provides, both materially and spiritually, for His people's needs.

5. Healer - In the verse "He restores my soul," God is portrayed as a healer. He not only takes care of physical needs but also restores the soul, bringing peace and healing to emotional and spiritual wounds.
